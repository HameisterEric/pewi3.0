The CSR2 calculates the corn suitability value as: \(CSR2 = S-M-W-F-D +/- EJ\) where
  • S is the taxonomic subgroup class of the soil
  • M is the family particle size class (soil particle composition)
  • W is a function of the soil’s water holding capacity
  • F is the field condition factor, including slope, erosion class, flooding, and topsoil thickness
  • D incorporates soil depth and “T factor,” or soil loss tolerance
  • EJ is an expert judgment factor accounting for series abnormalities such as textural extremes or high bulk density (ISU 2015 CSR2)
