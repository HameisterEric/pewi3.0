The equation is $$\sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^{m_i} A_{ij} * YB_{ij}[Wood] * YP_{ij}$$ where \(YB_{ij}[Wood] = CM *YB_{ij}[ConventionalWood]\) and \(CM\) is a conservation multiplier that is equal to .7 in conservation forest and 1.0 in conventional forest. \(YP_{ij}\) is equal to 1 in all precipitation scenarios. Yield on a per unit basis is maximized in Nodaway 220, Downs 162D2, Clarion 138B, Tama 120C2, Tama 120B, Gara-Armstrong 993E2, Nicollet 55, and Muscatine 119 soils and minimized in Canisteo 507, Coland 135, and Okoboji 90 soils.
