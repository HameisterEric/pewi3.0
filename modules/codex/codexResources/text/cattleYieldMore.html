The yield equation for head of cattle is given by $$\sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^{m_i} A_{ij} * YB_{ij}[Cattle] * YP_{ij}$$ where \(YB_{ij}[Cattle] = YB_{ij}[Alfalfa] * SU / (DI * GS)\).
Note: ISPAID hay yield data is used to model both Alfalfa and Grass Hay in PEWI, meaning \(YB_{ij}[Alfalfa] \equiv YB_{ij}[GrassHay]\).

  • \(SU\) is the seasonal utilization rate, which is equal to .35 in permanent pasture and .55 in rotational grazing.
  • \(DI\) is average daily intake, estimated at 3% of live bodyweight of .544 Mg, equal to .0163 Mg/day/animal.
  • \(GS\) is the grazing season length, April 15 to November 1, which is 200 days.

Cattle production in PEWI is maximized in any of the middle five precipitation scenarios, with all grid cells in rotational grazing land use type. Yield on a per-unit basis is maximized in Tama 120B, followed closely by Muscatine 119, Tama 120C2, and Nicollet 55. Per-unit yield is minimized in Buckney 1636 soils, Gara-Armstrong 993E2 soils, and Okoboji 90 soils, and it is low in extreme precipitation years of 62.4 cm/year or 114.6 cm/year.
